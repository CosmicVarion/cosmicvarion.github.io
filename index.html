About Me

I am a computer engineering student at the University at Buffalo. In the fall of 2014, I arrived to UB with virtually no programming experience. Under a year later, I became an undergraduate teaching assistant for Introduction to Computer Science I, a course that covers the fundamentals of object oriented programming in Java. As well, I was able to join the team at the Buffalo Neuroimaging Analysis Center (BNAC), developing software solutions for diverse challenges in MRI image processing. I continue to enjoy involvement in research in human connectomics on the team at the BNAC and teaching the ideational concepts of computer science. More recently, I worked at Empire Genomics where I learned web development and developed software tools on a team to streamline the clinical laboratory.
